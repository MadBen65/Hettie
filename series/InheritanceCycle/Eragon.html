Eragon (2003)

In Eragon a 15 year old farm boy named Eragon finds a mysterious sapphire blue stone in the Spine, a range of untamed mountains near his home. The stone hatches into a dragon, whom he names Saphira. This discovery thrusts Eragon into a world of magic, power, and destiny as he becomes a Dragon Rider. He must navigate the challenges of this new life while facing the evil king Galbatorix and his forces. The story explores themes of courage, friendship, and the struggle between good and evil.

Quick Facts

  • Eragon's first spell is "Brisingr," which means "fire" in the ancient language.
  • Eragon's sword is named Zar'roc, which means "misery" in the ancient language.
  • Saphira's hatching marks Eragon as a new Dragon Rider.
  • Brom secretly trains Eragon in sword fighting and the ancient language.
  • Murtagh becomes one of Eragon's key allies during his early journey.
  • The story ends with Eragon joining the Varden to fight Galbatorix.
